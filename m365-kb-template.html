Microsoft 365 — What’s the Difference?
A plain-English guide to mailboxes, groups & more
Not sure which type of mailbox or group you need? Use this guide to find the right fit — or open a ticket and we’ll help!
Personal (User) Mailbox
Your own email inbox — like a personal locker at work. Only you have the key. Comes with your own calendar, contacts, and storage. Requires a Microsoft 365 license.
One person Has inbox Needs license
Shared Mailbox
A shared inbox a whole team can access — like a front-desk filing cabinet. Common examples: info@, helpdesk@, or hr@. Multiple people can read and reply without logging in as a separate user. No dedicated license needed in most cases.
Whole team Has inbox Usually no license
Distribution Group (Distro List)
Simply a mailing list. Email one address and it auto-forwards a copy to everyone on the list. Nothing is stored — there is no inbox to open. Think of it like a group megaphone: one message, many recipients.
Mailing list No inbox No license needed
Microsoft 365 Group
A power bundle. One group gives you a shared inbox and a shared calendar and shared file storage and a Teams channel — all linked automatically. This is the hidden engine running behind most Teams and SharePoint setups, even when you cannot see it.
Teams / Projects Inbox + extras No extra license
Teams Channel
An open group chat room for a team or topic. Everyone on the team can see every message. Backed by a Microsoft 365 Group behind the scenes.
Visible to whole team
Teams Chat (Direct Message)
Like texting — a private conversation between you and one person or a small group. Not stored in email. Not visible to anyone else.
Private only
Room / Equipment Mailbox
A mailbox owned by a thing — a conference room, projector, or company vehicle. Invite it to a meeting and it auto-accepts or declines based on availability.
No license needed
Contact
Not a mailbox at all — just an address book entry. Used so outside vendors or partners show up in the company directory. No login, no inbox.
External people only
